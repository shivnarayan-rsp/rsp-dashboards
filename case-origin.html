Why case origin matters

When a technician creates a case themselves — or a service controller creates it on behalf of a phone call — they control the timestamp. This means the clock starts when they decide to log it, not when the customer actually had a problem. Over time this inflates response time performance and masks real downtime.

The goal is to increase the proportion of cases created by the customer directly (Email, Web/Portal) or by automated systems (@remote meter alerts, 3manager alerts). These origins are objective — the timestamp cannot be manipulated.
