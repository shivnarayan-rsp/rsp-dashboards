The honest picture

The callback rate of 30.9% is the most important number to address. Every third closed case is coming back — this is the single biggest driver of wasted tech time, extended asset downtime, and customer dissatisfaction. The weekly conversations and coaching patterns in this guide are designed specifically to move this number down.

01

Weekly rhythm

Three cadences keep the service operation running. Daily catches what is burning. Weekly is the management layer — review, conversations, coaching. Monthly is strategic — scores, bonus, trend analysis.

Daily — 10 min
Morning ops check
  • Check Asset Downtime — new Tech-delay cases overnight?
  • Check Open Cases — new escalations or Stop Credit?
  • Any case stuck in Work Order Issued >24h? Message the tech directly.
  • Any new callback? Note asset serial for the weekly conversation.
Weekly — 90 min total
Service review + conversations
  • Open Leaderboard — who moved up or down?
  • Open Conversations — check prompts before each call
  • 1:1 with each tech: 10 min. Score manual KPIs. Log notes and commitment.
  • Review Callbacks — any repeat assets to address?
  • Check Case Origin — any techs self-logging this week?
Monthly — 60 min
Score, bonus & trend review
  • Finalise all manual KPI scores in Conversations for the month
  • Review leaderboard ranking — confirm A/B/C/D categories
  • Determine bonus eligibility (A/B = eligible, C = review, D = PIP)
  • Check Parts & Revenue — SLA spend vs T&M
  • Share summary with Shiv: callback trend, FTF trend, any escalations

Context on current scores

With callback rates at 21–47%, the Repeat Call Rate KPI (10 pts, target ≤5%) will score near zero for everyone right now. Even a tech with excellent FTF and full PM compliance will struggle to reach B category. Use this as evidence for a team-wide callback reduction focus — not as a punitive measure while the baseline is this far from target.

05

Running the weekly conversation

Each 1:1 takes 10–15 minutes. Open the Conversations page before you start. The coaching prompts at the bottom of each tech's card are generated from their actual Salesforce data — read them before you call.

Open
1
min
Check in on last week's commitment
Open with the follow-up field from last week. Ask directly: "Last week you said you were going to [X]. How did that go?" Acknowledge if they delivered. If they didn't, ask what got in the way — don't skip this step. If commitments are never followed up, they stop meaning anything.
Data
4
min
Walk through the Salesforce metrics together
Share screen or read the numbers aloud. Be specific — not "your callback rate is high" but "you had 11 callbacks this month from 29 cases." Then open the callbacks page filtered for their territory:
  • "Your FTF is 78% — we need 85%. That is about 2 extra cases needing a return visit."
  • "Let us look at the callbacks — here are yours for this month. Any of these look familiar?"
  • "Response time showing 3h median — that is inside target."
Coach
5
min
Work the coaching focus from the prompts
The auto-generated prompts at the bottom of each card tell you what to focus on based on the data. Use open questions — avoid telling them what to do:
  • "When you go back to the same machine a second time — what is typically the reason?"
  • "Are you getting enough time to diagnose properly, or are you being rushed to the next job?"
  • "Is there a parts availability issue on a specific model you keep seeing?"
Score
3
min
Score the six manual KPIs
Move the sliders on the Conversations page for: job audit, CSAT, report timeliness, PM compliance, safety. Be honest — if you do not have evidence for a score, default to mid-range rather than giving full marks automatically. The score summary table at the top of the page updates live.
Open Conversations →
Close
2
min
Get one specific commitment and log it
End with a single measurable commitment. Not "try to do better on callbacks" — that is unmeasurable. Instead: "For the next three calls on Bulk Shop's IM 5000, I will spend 30 minutes on diagnostics before ordering any parts." Log it in the commitment field. This is what you open with next week.
Saving scores

Click "Save all scores & notes" at the top of the Conversations page after each session. Data is saved by month in your browser. Once the Supabase migration is complete, scores will sync server-side and be accessible from any device, with trend lines available after 2–3 months of data.

06

Coaching patterns

The most common performance patterns you will see in the data, and the questions to ask for each. Ask and listen — do not diagnose or prescribe.

Urgent — address immediately
Callback rate > 30%
  • Walk me through your last three callbacks. What was the root cause each time?
  • Are you ordering parts before fully diagnosing the fault?
  • Is there a specific machine model that keeps coming back? Let us look at the serial.
  • Are you getting enough time on-site, or are you being pushed to move on?
If above 30% for two months, schedule a joint site visit — you attend alongside the tech to observe diagnostic process.
Urgent — address immediately
FTF rate < 70%
  • Of cases needing a second visit — were any parts return visits or genuine re-diagnoses?
  • Are Work Orders capturing what was actually done? A parts return should not count against FTF if logged correctly.
  • Is there a knowledge gap on any specific machine model?
  • Do you have access to the service manuals and technical bulletins you need?
Check the tech's case list sorted by Work Order count. Cases with 3+ WOs are where FTF is being lost — look for patterns.
Watch — discuss this week
Avg days to close > 10 days
  • Are cases being left open after work is done, or is there genuine delay?
  • What is your process for invoicing and closing — are you doing it same day?
  • Are there cases in Processing Invoice that have been there over a week? What is blocking them?
Filter Asset Downtime for their territory. Tech-delay cases open more than 72h are their responsibility — go through these together.
Watch — discuss this week
Self-logging cases (Technician origin)
  • How are customers currently contacting you when they have a problem?
  • When you create the case yourself on arrival — how long after the customer called did you get there?
  • Can we give the account the email address or portal link to log directly?
Technician-origin cases inflate response time numbers. The goal is objective origins — not to punish the tech, but to make the data trustworthy for everyone.
Watch — discuss this week
Same asset appearing repeatedly
  • This machine has had [X] callbacks this year. What is your read on its condition?
  • Has the customer been told the machine may need replacing?
  • Is there a CPC contract on this asset? Should we be raising a T&M quote?
Check the repeat assets table on the Leaderboard — serials are linked to the Salesforce asset record. A machine with 5+ calls in a quarter is a candidate for an account conversation about replacement.
Positive — acknowledge and reinforce
FTF ≥ 85% and callbacks ≤ 10%
  • What is your diagnostic process when you arrive on site? Walk me through it.
  • What do you do differently when you are unsure about a fault?
  • Would you be willing to share your approach with the rest of the team?
Ask them to articulate what they do well — this makes it a skill they own, not luck. Recognition in the monthly summary to Shiv is warranted.
07

Data hygiene

The dashboard is only as good as the data in Salesforce. These behaviours must be consistent across the team for the metrics to be trustworthy.

Daily — technician responsibilitiesOwner: each tech
Close cases the same day work is completedLeaving cases open inflates avg days to close. If waiting on invoice approval, set status to Processing Invoice — not Work Order Issued.
Tech
Complete Work Order notes before leaving siteFault description, parts used or ordered, resolution method. This is what the job audit score is based on. Sparse notes = lost score.
Tech
Log the correct Work Type on every caseService Call, Parts Fitting, Toner Delivery, Asset Maintenance — these filter separately across the dashboard. A Service Call logged as Toner Delivery disappears from performance metrics entirely.
Tech
Do not create a case if one already existsIf the customer emailed or the SC already logged it, do not create a duplicate. A duplicate case with a later timestamp makes response time look worse than it was.
Tech
Daily — service controller responsibilitiesOwner: Service Controller
Log phone calls immediately — not at end of dayEvery hour of delay inflates apparent response time. If the customer called at 9am and the case is logged at 4pm, the tech can never meet a 4h response target even if they arrived by noon.
SC
Assign territory at case creationCases without a territory appear as Unassigned across all dashboard pages and don't count toward territory KPIs. Currently 8 open cases have no territory assigned.
SC
Update status when blocked externallyCases stuck in Work Order Issued look like tech delay on the Asset Downtime page. If the job is genuinely waiting on parts or customer approval, update the status so the delay is classified correctly.
SC
Weekly — manager reviewOwner: Sridhar
Review open cases older than 30 days — close or escalateCheck Asset Downtime filtered by Over 30 days. Any case this old needs a clear owner and next action, or should be closed if work is complete.

Spot-check one tech's Work Orders each weekClick a tech name on the leaderboard → review 5 recent cases → check Work Order notes. This is the data behind the Job Audit score. Rotate which tech you check.

Escalation triggers

Some situations need to move beyond the weekly conversation. These are the thresholds that trigger a formal escalation to Shiv, HR, or the account manager.

!
D category for two consecutive months → Shiv + formal plan
A score below 60 for two months means the weekly conversations are not producing change. Escalate to Shiv with the two-month score summary from the Conversations page. A formal performance improvement plan should be initiated with HR support. Continue weekly conversations and document everything.
!
Callback rate > 40% in any month → joint site visit
Above 40% is not a data anomaly — roughly every other job is coming back. Schedule a joint site visit to observe the tech's diagnostic and repair process in person. This is coaching, not performance management. Bring the relevant case numbers and asset serials from the Callbacks page.
