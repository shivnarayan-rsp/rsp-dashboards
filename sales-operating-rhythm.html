The Suite at a Glance

This dashboard suite is designed to support one outcome: Avi Sharma closes more deals, clears the SLOB equipment backlog, and builds a team of reps who maintain their own pipeline hygiene. Every page maps to a specific moment in the week. Total active time: approximately 90 minutes per week.

Manager expectation: this suite is not for passive reporting. It is used to identify gaps, decide where Avi needs to intervene, and drive follow-through each week.

Operating standard: review → decide → act → follow up.

Run the Playbook: use the same rhythm and standards every week. Nimble like a Cheetah: act early when pipeline health or rep behaviour starts to drift. Cross the Finish Line: close commitments, not just conversations. Deliver the Gold Standard: use the data to lift sales quality, not just measure it. Sharpen the Blade: use repeated issues to improve judgement, process, and pipeline discipline.

The Weekly Rhythm

The weekly rhythm is where Avi turns dashboard data into management action. The standard each week is simple: know the number, name the gap, agree the action, and follow it up.

Monday
Set the week
15 minutes · solo
  • Open the Dashboard. Read the WIG banner — are both WIGs on track, and which one needs the most intervention this week?
  • Check pipeline coverage ratio. Is it above 3×? If not, decide whether this is a pipeline volume issue, deal quality issue, or conversion issue.
  • Identify which territory is furthest behind quota attainment and what support or pressure is needed there this week.
  • Note the two reps you need to focus most attention on this week based on their attainment %. They will be the top cards on the Rep Scorecard page. This is where Avi chooses where to coach, where to challenge, and where to step in.
→ sales-dashboard.html
Tuesday
1:1s — Rep 1 & 2
30 min per rep
  • Before each meeting: open Rep Scorecards, expand that rep's card, and identify the one issue that matters most.
  • During the meeting: walk the 5-part agenda (see below). Keep the discussion anchored in facts and action.
  • Rep opens Salesforce on their own device to update next step dates in real time.
  • Capture 2–3 commitments using the card's commitments section, with clear dates and clear ownership.
  • Copy to clipboard and paste into Teams or email as your record. Commitments should be visible and followed up next week.
→ sales-rep-scorecard.html
Wednesday
1:1s — Rep 3 & 4
30 min per rep
  • Same rhythm as Tuesday. Keep the standard consistent across reps.
  • Mid-week check: is anyone's SLOB clearance progress visible in the pipeline? Check if any SLOB machine deals have been created in Salesforce. If not, decide whether the issue is focus, effort, or deal viability.
→ sales-rep-scorecard.html
Thursday
1:1s — Remaining reps
30 min per rep
  • Complete remaining weekly 1:1s and close any gaps left from earlier meetings.
  • If a rep has zero logged events or tasks this week, address directly: "What have you been doing with your time? It's not showing in Salesforce." Treat this as a real management issue, not an admin detail.
→ sales-rep-scorecard.html
Friday
Close the week
10 minutes · solo
  • Open Dashboard. Check Sales This Week vs last Friday and note whether the week created real movement.
  • Did next step compliance improve from Monday? It should move upward each week. If it did not, decide what follow-up is needed next week.
  • Note any deals that closed — update the WIG 1 mental model and recognise which behaviours drove the result.
  • Are we on track to hit the SLOB clearance deadline? If not, identify what needs escalation now rather than later.
→ sales-dashboard.html

The 1:1 Conversation Guide

This is the agenda for every weekly rep 1:1. It runs for approximately 30 minutes. Keep it structured — an unstructured 1:1 defaults to the rep talking about whatever they are comfortable with, which is rarely the thing that needs the most attention. The purpose is to review facts, test ownership, agree actions, and follow up.

Weekly 1:1 Agenda — 30 minutes
Run in this order. Do not skip steps. Open Rep Scorecards on your device before sitting down. The standard is facts first, action second, excuses last.
1
Set the context — the WIG number and the performance gap
2 minutes
Read the quota vs Closed Won number from the rep's scorecard card aloud. No discussion needed — just establish the shared facts and the gap that needs to be closed.
"You're at NZ$X of your NZ$Y target. We have Z working days left this month. Here's the gap, and here's what we need to focus on today."
2
Walk the red deals and test deal ownership
10 minutes
Click "View all [Name]'s open deals" from the scorecard card — this opens the Deal List filtered to that rep, sorted by most flags first. For each flagged deal in the top 5: ask two questions only. Use the answers to decide whether the issue is deal quality, rep follow-through, or false pipeline.
"What's the current status on this one?" then "What is your next action, and what date will you do it by?"
The rep updates the next step date in Salesforce on their device in the room. Do not accept "I'll update it later." Cross the Finish Line applies inside the meeting, not after it.
3
Lead measure check-in and weekly behaviour focus
5 minutes
Look at the four lead measure bars on the scorecard card. The goal is not to shame — it is to agree on one thing the rep will improve this week and to make the expectation explicit.
"Your next step compliance is X%. Which three deals are you going to update a next step on before Friday?"
4
Activity review and evidence check
5 minutes
Check the activity counts on the scorecard card (events, tasks logged). If the numbers are low, this is the coaching moment — it means either the rep is not active, or they are not logging. Avi needs to decide which of those two problems he is dealing with.
"Salesforce shows X events this week. Does that reflect your actual week?" If no: "Let's update it now — what did you do that isn't logged?"
Low logging is itself a performance issue: if it's not in Salesforce, it didn't happen for forecasting purposes. Repeated low logging should not stay as an informal reminder.
5
Commitments — close the meeting and lock accountability
3 minutes
Type 2–3 specific commitments into the commitments section of the rep's scorecard card. Each commitment has a specific action, a specific date, and an obvious test of completion.
"Before we finish — you've committed to: (1) follow up Ministry of Finance by Wednesday, (2) update next step dates on your top 5 Samson deals by Friday, (3) create a Salesforce opportunity for the SLOB IM C6000 you're offering to ANZ. I'll open next week's 1:1 by reading these back to you."
Click "Copy commitments to clipboard" and paste into your Teams message or email to the rep. Commitments only matter if they are followed up.
Manager action: use the 1:1 to decide whether the issue is coaching, capability, effort, pipeline quality, or performance management. Do not leave repeated patterns sitting as vague "support" conversations.

Monthly Pipeline Purge

Run on the first Monday of each month. Budget 20 minutes per rep across the week's 1:1s. The purpose is to keep the pipeline number meaningful — 83% aged pipeline means the NZ$2.58M total is significantly overstated. This is not admin cleanup; it is sales management discipline.

  1. Open Deal List. Set "Status" filter to "Aged >90 days." You will see 624 deals.
  2. For each rep's 1:1 that week: open the Deal List filtered to their name AND aged >90 days.
  3. For each aged deal, ask one question: "Is this deal still real?" If yes: set a new close date and a next step date. If no: mark it Closed Lost in Salesforce. The manager's job is to push for an honest answer, not protect inflated pipeline.
  4. Every deal that leaves the pipeline (whether closed won or closed lost) improves the coverage ratio accuracy. The goal is not to lose deals — it is to have an honest pipeline number. False comfort is worse than bad news.
  5. Target: reduce aged pipeline from 83% to below 40% over three monthly purge cycles. If the number does not move, the issue is no longer just hygiene — it is manager follow-through.

Quarterly Maintenance

At the start of each quarter, update the following in the dashboard script (sales-dashboard.html DATA object) to keep the WIG numbers accurate. Accurate inputs are part of Run the Playbook — reliable data supports reliable decisions.

  • Update QUOTA array with new ForecastingQuota values from Salesforce (StartDate = new quarter start)
  • Update BUDGET.mtd and BUDGET.ytd with confirmed territory budget figures from Minoo/Pradeep
  • Run a fresh Salesforce MCP session to pull updated pipeline, activity, and closed won data into the DATA objects so Avi is managing from current facts, not stale assumptions
  • After BC migration (November 2026), transition data source from Salesforce MCP + SyncHub to Power BI embedded reports
