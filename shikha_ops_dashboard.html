EOS + 4DX at RSP

RSP uses 4DX (4 Disciplines of Execution) for operational focus — WIGs, lead measures, and the weekly Scorecard. EOS provides the meeting cadence (L10) and accountability structure (Rocks, Issues List, IDS). The two frameworks complement each other: 4DX tells you what to measure and score weekly; EOS tells you how to run the conversations around those numbers.

WIGs replace "Rocks" from standard EOS. The Scorecard tab is your weekly L10 Scorecard. Issues that appear on this dashboard and can't be resolved by Shikha alone go on the L10 Issues List for IDS. The operating standard is simple: review → decide → act → follow up.

01

Weekly rhythm

Three cadences. Daily ops keeps things from falling through — a 10-minute check to catch anything burning before it becomes a problem. Weekly is the primary accountability layer — Scorecard, WIG progress, decisions, and L10 prep. Monthly is strategic — WIG trajectory, what's working, what needs to go on next quarter's WIG list, and what needs escalation or structural change.

Daily — 10 min
Morning ops check
  • Check Orders tab — any new stale orders overnight? Did RSP's count move? Decide what must be cleared today.
  • Check BMS tab — any new open billing transactions? Tonga is the priority. Confirm what action is required today, not just what is open.
  • Check Invoice Drill-Down — any DRAFT invoices ageing past 7 days? Identify the oldest record and who owns closing it.
  • Flag anything red to the relevant person before 9am. Don't absorb it yourself. Confirm owner, timing, and next check point.
Weekly — L10 Tuesday
Scorecard prep + L10
  • Monday pm: open Scorecard tab — know every lead measure before you walk in, and know which ones need action from you this week.
  • For every red tile: one sentence ready — what caused it, what you're doing this week, and what outcome you expect before next L10.
  • Open WIGs tab — are progress bars moving? Any WIG stuck 2+ weeks becomes an Issue, not just a repeated update.
  • If payroll week: open Payroll tracker — check cycle progress before the meeting and identify the current bottleneck.
  • After L10: update any to-dos assigned to you. Same day. Cross the Finish Line applies to meeting actions too.
Monthly — 60 min
WIG trajectory review
  • Review all three WIGs against June 2026 targets — on track, at risk, or off track? Name clearly which require intervention, escalation, or a lead-measure reset.
  • Payroll: review avg processing time trend across all four entities and identify the repeated cause of delay.
  • BMS: review Tonga billing completion trend — is it moving up week by week? If not, decide what changes in process, ownership, or support are required.
  • Whirlwind tab: are delegated tasks actually landing with the right owners, or is work drifting back on to Shikha?
  • Flag anything to Shiv that needs a quarterly WIG adjustment, additional resource, or a structural ownership decision.

03

Running the L10 Scorecard

The L10 Scorecard is a 5-minute agenda item — not a reporting session. Each lead measure gets a green or red. Red tiles get one sentence: cause and action. The goal is clarity, not justification. The Scorecard is not a performance review — it is a weekly calibration of whether the team is on trajectory for the WIGs. The purpose is to trigger decisions and follow-through.

Green tile

At or above target. Say it aloud, acknowledge the team member responsible, move on. No discussion needed. The L10 is not the place to explain green tiles. Use green tiles to reinforce what good looks like.

Red tile

Off target. One sentence: what caused it, what action you're taking this week. If red for 2 consecutive weeks with no movement — add to Issues List for IDS. Do not accept red tiles as normal. Repeated red without consequence weakens ownership.

The 4DX principle on lead measures

Lag measures tell you if you won. Lead measures tell you if you're going to win. The Scorecard tracks lead measures — the things Shikha controls this week that predict whether the WIGs will be hit by June. If a lead measure is consistently green but the WIG progress bar isn't moving, the lead measure is wrong. Raise it at the quarterly WIG review. Sharpen the Blade applies here: refine the measure when it is no longer driving the outcome.
